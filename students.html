STUDENTS

Who Should Apply

The ACCA qualification is for anyone who wants a rewarding career in finance, accountancy or management. You can join the ACCA qualification whether you have completed 10+2, bachelors, masters or hold accountancy qualifications from other bodies. The ACCA qualification has different entry routes so depending on your previous qualification; you can be awarded exemptions accordingly. On the other hand, if you have no previous formal qualification, you can enroll into ACCA as an FIA (Foundations in Accountancy) student and still be eligible to proceed with your ACCA qualification.

Pre-Requisites

Minimum entry criteria for ACCA in India listed below

  1. No formal qualifications

    If you have no formal qualifications, you can begin the ACCA qualification by enrolling as Foundations In Accountancy Student.

  2. After School

    Two A Levels and three GCSEs (or their equivalent) These need to be in five separate subjects including English and maths.

    OR

    If you hold any of the following certificates providing passes are held in 5 subjects (at least 3 in Year XII) including English and Mathematics / Accounts, mark of 65% in at least 2 subjects and over 50% on the others

    • 10+2
    • India School Certificate
    • Intermediate Certificate
    • Higher School Certificate
    • Higher Secondary Certificate
    • All India Senior School Certificate
    • Pre-University Course
  3. Under Graduates/Post Graduates/Other Professional Qualifications

    If you have completed your bachelors, masters or hold qualification from other bodies, then you will be entitled to exemptions. To check how many exemptions you can claim, please click on the link below and enter your institute name. Follow the steps thereafter to find out your exemptions.

    link However, please note that until you are registered with ACCA as a student the exemptions will not be finalized or reflected on your account.
